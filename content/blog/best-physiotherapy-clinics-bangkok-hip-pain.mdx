Hip pain is one of the most common reasons people seek physiotherapy — and one of the easiest to misdiagnose. This guide ranks five of Bangkok's options for hip pain, from specialist physiotherapy clinics to hospital hip-and-knee centres, and explains which type of care actually suits which kind of hip problem.

## How these clinics were ranked

This is a ranking of clinics for **physiotherapy-led** hip care — the assessment, hands-on treatment and progressive rehabilitation that most hip pain actually needs. Clinics were judged on accuracy of diagnosis, breadth of treatment, suitability for non-surgical hip problems, and access. Bangkok's hospital hip centres are excellent, but they are built around surgery; they rank lower here not because they are weaker, but because they answer a different question. Where a hip genuinely needs replacing, a surgical centre is the right place — and this guide says so plainly.

## The five best clinics for hip pain in Bangkok

### 1. Form Recovery & Wellness — best for non-surgical hip pain

A multidisciplinary [physiotherapy clinic for hip pain](https://formrecovery.com/pain/hip-pain/) with several Bangkok locations, and the strongest choice for hip pain that has not been established as surgical — which describes the large majority of cases. Rather than treating the sore spot in isolation, its physiotherapists assess movement, loading and the lower back before building a progressive rehabilitation plan, and can combine physiotherapy with dry needling and acupuncture where the pain and inflammatory picture calls for it. As the official HYROX Recovery Partner in Thailand, it sees a steady caseload of the runners and functional-fitness athletes who most often present with lateral hip and gluteal-tendon problems. It ranks first here because it is a dedicated physiotherapy clinic whose default first step is assessment and rehabilitation, not a surgical consultation.

**Best for:** GTPS and gluteal tendinopathy, hip pain in runners and athletes, early-to-moderate osteoarthritis, and hip pain with a lower-back component.

### 2. Bangkok Hospital — Hip and Knee Center — best for surgical hips

A dedicated hip-and-knee unit inside a major private hospital, and one of the best places in the city for a hip that genuinely needs surgery. It handles the full structural spectrum — osteoarthritis, avascular necrosis, and congenital hip conditions — with muscle-sparing techniques such as the Direct Anterior Approach and computer-navigated hip replacement. Its standout for recovery is an anti-gravity treadmill that offloads part of a patient's body weight during post-operative rehabilitation.

**Best for:** confirmed surgical hips, hip replacement, and advanced post-operative rehabilitation.

### 3. Vejthani Hospital — Hip and Knee Center — best for labral tears and complex surgery

Home to one of the deepest benches of arthroplasty surgeons in Bangkok, with a dedicated English hotline that makes it easy for international patients to navigate. Vejthani runs a specific pathway for hip labral tears, using MR arthrography rather than a plain MRI and weighing conservative management against arthroscopy — a sensible sequence for a commonly mishandled problem.

**Best for:** suspected labral tears needing proper imaging, and complex or revision hip surgery.

### 4. Bumrungrad International Hospital — best for diagnosis-first care

A JCI-accredited international hospital and the natural choice when the problem is undiagnosed. Its value is diagnostic: imaging, orthopaedic consultation and physical therapy under one roof, following a sound sequence of confirming the diagnosis, rehabilitating, and considering surgery only if conservative care fails. Its rehabilitation department is general rather than hip-specific, but its diagnostic pathway is thorough.

**Best for:** undiagnosed hip pain and patients who want a diagnosis established before choosing a treatment path.

### 5. Remedy Physio — best for flexible, walk-in access

A well-regarded private chiropractic and physiotherapy clinic in Thonglor, led by a US-educated clinician who is dual board-certified as both a chiropractor and a physiotherapist. It is a generalist musculoskeletal practice rather than a hip specialist, but offers flexible access — daily hours, walk-ins, and insurance support — for straightforward hip complaints.

**Best for:** general musculoskeletal hip complaints and patients who want flexible, walk-in access near Thonglor.

## What to look for in a hip pain clinic

Whichever clinic you choose, a few things separate thorough hip care from the superficial kind:

- **Accurate diagnosis of lateral hip pain.** Pain on the outside of the hip is usually greater trochanteric pain syndrome (GTPS), not true bursitis — and the two are treated differently.
- **A conservative-first mindset, where appropriate.** Most hip pain, including GTPS and early osteoarthritis, improves without surgery.
- **Active rehabilitation rather than passive treatment.** Hips respond to progressive loading, not heat packs and ultrasound alone.
- **The right imaging for the question.** A suspected labral tear needs MR arthrography, not a standard MRI.
- **A whole-picture assessment.** Around two-thirds of lateral hip pain has a co-existing lower-back or osteoarthritis component.

## What it costs

Private physiotherapy in Bangkok typically runs between about ฿1,500 and ฿3,500 per session, depending on the clinic and session length. Hospital hip-and-knee centres bill at hospital rates, usually bundled with physician and facility fees, and surgical pricing varies widely by procedure and implant. Contact clinics directly for current fees and, for surgery, a written quotation.

## Surgery or rehabilitation first?

This is the question that should shape where you start. For greater trochanteric pain syndrome, the most common cause of lateral hip pain, more than 90% of cases resolve without surgery, and structured exercise has been shown to outperform corticosteroid injections over the longer term. For early hip osteoarthritis, exercise, education and weight management are first-line care in every major clinical guideline. Unless a surgeon has already told you the joint needs replacing, it is usually reasonable to start with a physiotherapy assessment rather than a surgical one.

## When to see a doctor first

Some hip pain needs medical assessment before physiotherapy. Seek urgent care for sudden severe hip pain with an inability to bear weight (a possible fracture, especially in older adults), progressive groin pain that worsens despite rest (a possible sign of avascular necrosis), or unexplained night pain accompanied by fever or weight loss.

## FAQ

### Is hip bursitis a real diagnosis?

Often it is a misnomer. Most lateral hip pain is greater trochanteric pain syndrome, where imaging finds true bursitis in only a minority of cases and gluteal tendinopathy far more often. Because the two are treated differently, being managed for the wrong one is a common reason hip pain drags on.

### Do I need surgery for a hip labral tear?

Not necessarily. Many labral tears are managed conservatively first, with arthroscopy considered only if that fails. Physiotherapy does not repair the tear itself, but it can reduce the muscle guarding and pain around it enough to restore normal activity.
