Bangkok is one of the world's leading destinations for IVF and fertility treatment — internationally trained specialists, modern labs, and costs a fraction of those in the US, UK or Australia. Thousands of couples and individuals travel here each year for IVF, ICSI and egg freezing, and many of the city's clinics are set up specifically for international patients.

## What to Expect at a Bangkok Fertility Clinic

Your first visit is a consultation and fertility work-up — hormone blood tests, an ultrasound, and (for couples) a semen analysis. The specialist reviews the results, explains your options, and outlines a treatment timeline before you commit.

A full IVF cycle typically runs over 4–6 weeks: ovarian stimulation with daily injections, egg retrieval under sedation, fertilisation in the lab (often via ICSI), and an embryo transfer a few days later. Egg freezing follows the same path up to retrieval. Good clinics track everything closely and explain each step — important when you may be coordinating part of the process from abroad.

**What to bring:**
- Any previous fertility test results or treatment records
- Your marriage certificate (required under Thai law for IVF — see below)
- A list of current medications

## How Much Does IVF Cost in Bangkok?

Prices vary by clinic, the specialist, and how much medication your cycle needs.

| Treatment | Typical price |
|---|---|
| Consultation & fertility testing | ฿2,000–10,000 |
| IUI (per cycle) | ฿15,000–35,000 |
| IVF / ICSI (per cycle, excl. medication) | ฿250,000–450,000 |
| Egg freezing (per cycle) | ฿120,000–200,000 |
| Embryo / egg storage (per year) | ฿10,000–30,000 |

Medication can add ฿60,000–150,000 to an IVF cycle depending on your protocol. Most clinics quote packages — ask exactly what's included (and what isn't) before you start.

## Choosing the Right Clinic

### English-speaking specialists and international support

Fertility treatment is detailed and emotional, and you'll have a lot of questions. Prioritise clinics with English-speaking specialists and international-patient coordinators who can handle scheduling around your travel. Most dedicated IVF centres in central Bangkok are set up for exactly this.

### Success rates and lab quality

Ask about success rates for your age group specifically — not the clinic's headline number. The embryology lab matters as much as the doctor: consistent results come from a well-run lab with experienced embryologists. Established, IVF-focused centres tend to be a safer bet than general hospitals adding fertility as a side service.

### Thai law and eligibility

Thailand's fertility law is strict and worth understanding before you travel. IVF is legally available to **married couples**, and clinics will require a valid marriage certificate. **Commercial surrogacy is banned**, and **sex selection is prohibited** except for specific medical reasons. Reputable clinics follow these rules closely — any clinic offering to bypass them is a serious red flag.

## FAQ

### Is IVF in Bangkok as good as back home?

Many Bangkok fertility specialists trained internationally and work in modern labs with experienced embryologists, and success rates at leading clinics are comparable to those in Western countries — at a fraction of the cost. As always, quality varies, so choose an established, IVF-focused centre with transparent success data.

### Can single women or same-sex couples do IVF in Thailand?

Thai law restricts IVF to married heterosexual couples, and clinics require a marriage certificate. Single people and same-sex couples are not currently eligible under Thai regulations. If this applies to you, discuss your situation directly with a clinic before making any travel plans.

### How long do I need to stay in Bangkok for IVF?

A full cycle spans roughly 4–6 weeks, but many international patients split it — initial testing remotely or on a short first trip, then a longer stay of around 2–3 weeks around stimulation, egg retrieval and transfer. Your clinic can plan the timeline around your travel.

### Will my insurance cover IVF?

Most insurance policies exclude fertility treatment, so IVF is usually paid out of pocket. Bangkok's lower prices are a large part of why patients travel here. Check your policy, and ask the clinic for itemised documentation if you intend to claim anything back.
