Phuket isn't just beaches and spas — the island has a growing base of genuine medical wellness clinics offering health screening, IV therapy, longevity and anti-aging care, and a strong traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) and acupuncture scene. This guide covers the *medical and clinical* side of wellness for expats, retirees and visitors — not resort day-spas or massage shops.

## What to Expect at a Phuket Wellness Clinic

A proper wellness clinic starts with assessment, not a treatment menu. Expect a consultation and baseline testing — bloodwork, sometimes body composition or a health screen — before a doctor recommends a plan. Because Phuket serves so many international patients, English-speaking (and often Russian-speaking) staff are common, and several clinics turn blood results around within hours.

From there, care might mean a comprehensive health check-up, a course of IV vitamin therapy, a longevity or hormone programme, or acupuncture and cupping for pain and recovery. Many clinics are used to short-stay visitors and will fit a plan into the time you have on the island.

**What to bring:**
- Any recent blood tests or medical records
- A list of current medications and supplements
- Your health goals — screening, energy, recovery, longevity

Wellness care in Phuket costs a fraction of equivalent private care in the West — part of why the island draws wellness-focused visitors and long-stay expats.

## Choosing the Right Clinic

### Doctor-led, not spa-led

The key distinction is whether a qualified doctor is behind the care. Prioritise clinics where physicians run consultations, order proper testing, and base recommendations on your results — not resort spas offering wellness "packages" without medical oversight.

### English-speaking and international support

Phuket's tourism means English is widely spoken at clinics, and many also handle Russian, Chinese and other languages. If you're planning a longevity programme or ongoing care, confirm the clinic has English-speaking doctors so nothing gets lost in translation.

### Location on the island

Phuket has no metro — it's drive-everywhere. Wellness clinics cluster around Phuket Town (Mueang), Kathu/Patong, Thalang and the Laguna/Cherngtalay area. Choose one near where you're staying, since screening and IV or acupuncture courses usually mean more than one visit.

## FAQ

### What counts as a "wellness" clinic here?

We focus on medical and clinical wellness — doctor-led clinics offering health screening, IV therapy, longevity and anti-aging care, hormone programmes, plus holistic care like acupuncture and TCM. We deliberately exclude resort day-spas and massage-only businesses, which aren't medical care.

### Do I need an appointment?

For most wellness clinics, yes — especially for health screening, IV programmes or acupuncture courses, which often run across several visits. Some acupuncture and TCM clinics operate by appointment only, so message ahead rather than walking in.

### Will staff speak English?

Most wellness clinics in Phuket's tourist areas have English-speaking staff, and many also handle Russian and Chinese given the island's visitor mix. Clinics listed as English-speaking on ThailandClinics have been confirmed to offer English-language service.

### Will my insurance cover wellness care?

Most insurance treats preventive wellness, screening and elective therapies as out-of-pocket rather than covered medical care. Bangkok and Phuket's lower prices are part of the appeal. Check your policy, and ask the clinic for itemised documentation if you intend to claim anything back.
