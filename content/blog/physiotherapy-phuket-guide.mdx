Phuket has a strong, tourist-ready physiotherapy scene — dozens of modern clinics, many with English-speaking therapists, and prices far below Western countries. Whether you're recovering from a motorbike spill, nursing a diving or watersports injury, managing office syndrome as a remote worker, or rehabbing after surgery, the island has the right care — often within a short drive of the main beaches.

## What to Expect at a Phuket Physio Clinic

Most private clinics follow international standards. A first session usually opens with a 15–20 minute assessment — history, movement tests, and a treatment plan explained before hands-on work begins. Because Phuket sees so many international patients, English-speaking therapists are common, especially in the tourist belt.

Sessions typically run **60 minutes** for the first visit and **45 minutes** for follow-ups, combining manual therapy, targeted exercises, and modalities like dry needling, shockwave or ultrasound. Many clinics are used to short-stay visitors and will compress a recovery plan into the days you have left on the island.

**What to bring:**
- Any previous imaging (MRI, X-ray reports)
- Your accident or insurance details, if it's a motorbike or sports injury
- Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing

## Choosing the Right Clinic

### English-speaking therapists

Phuket's tourism means English is widely spoken at private clinics, but it's still worth confirming. If you're explaining a complex injury or a long-standing condition, prioritise clinics that clearly list English-speaking therapists so nothing gets lost in translation.

### Condition-specific experience

Phuket therapists see a particular mix — motorbike and scooter injuries, diving and watersports strains, post-surgical rehab, and remote-worker office syndrome. If you have a specific issue, choose a clinic that mentions it. Some island clinics have built strong reputations for acute accident recovery or sports rehabilitation specifically.

### Location and getting there

Phuket has no Skytrain or metro — it's a drive-everywhere island. Choose a clinic near where you're staying (Rawai, Kata, Patong, Phuket Town or Thalang) with easy parking or scooter access, since you may be making repeat visits while managing an injury.

## FAQ

### I had a motorbike accident — can a physio clinic help?

Yes, and it's one of the most common reasons visitors see a physiotherapist in Phuket. For road-rash and soft-tissue injuries, a physio can assess and start rehab quickly. For anything involving suspected fractures or head injury, get checked at a hospital first, then use physiotherapy for the recovery phase.

### Do I need a doctor's referral?

No. You can book directly with any private physiotherapy clinic in Phuket without a referral. Hospital-based clinics may require one for insurance billing.

### Will therapists speak English?

Most private clinics in Phuket's tourist areas have English-speaking therapists, given how many international patients they see. Clinics listed as English-speaking on ThailandClinics have been confirmed to offer English-language service.

### Can I use my travel insurance?

Often, yes — especially for accident-related physiotherapy. Keep your accident documentation and receipts, and check whether your policy needs pre-authorisation. Many Phuket clinics are used to tourist insurance claims and can provide itemised paperwork.
