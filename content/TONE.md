# ThailandClinics — Content Tone & Style Guide

The canonical voice for **all** ThailandClinics content: blog pillars, cluster/best-of
posts, guides, clinic summaries, meta descriptions. The worked reference example is
[`content/blog/physiotherapy-bangkok-guide.mdx`](blog/physiotherapy-bangkok-guide.mdx) —
when in doubt, match it.

---

## The voice in one line

**A knowledgeable friend who has done the research for you** — warm, direct, specific,
and honest. We are an independent directory: *"We do the research to find the best."*
Never a salesperson.

## Who we're writing for

Expats and medical tourists navigating healthcare in Thailand — often new to the
country, English-speaking, deciding where to spend real money on their health. Respect
their intelligence; remove their uncertainty.

---

## Core rules

1. **Answer first, no preamble.** Lead every page and section with the answer. Never
   open with "In today's world…" or throat-clearing. (Also how AI engines extract you.)
2. **Second person, active voice.** "You'll find…", "Choose a clinic that…" — not
   "One may wish to consider…".
3. **Be specific, always.** Name conditions (GTPS, herniated disc, office syndrome),
   treatments (dry needling, HIFU, IV NAD+), doctors, districts, and real prices in ฿
   as ranges. If a sentence could describe any clinic in Bangkok, rewrite it.
4. **Be honest and balanced.** Say when another option is better (a hospital for a
   surgical hip; a spa is not a wellness clinic). Include red flags and "worth knowing"
   negatives. Trust is the whole product — this is YMYL healthcare.
5. **Short, plain sentences.** One idea each. No jargon, no corporate-speak. If a
   smart friend wouldn't say it over coffee, cut it.
6. **British English.** prioritise, rehabilitation, orthopaedic, specialise, centre,
   paediatric.
7. **Sentence case headings.** ALL CAPS only for eyebrow/tag labels.
8. **Back up numbers.** Stats get context and, where possible, a source. Prices are
   ranges ("฿1,500–3,500"), never invented precision.

## Structure we reuse

- **Ranked "best of" list:** `### N. [Clinic] — best for X`, a 2–4 sentence rationale
  grounded in specifics, and a bold **Best for:** line.
- **Costs:** a table with ฿ ranges, plus one honest caveat sentence.
- **What to look for / Red flags:** bulleted, bolded lead-in per point.
- **FAQ:** `## FAQ` then `### Question` (this format is what generates FAQPage schema).
- **Methodology/freshness note** on curated rankings + a visible "updated" date.

## Words & phrases to avoid

**Corporate/AI tells (never use):** modalities, arsenal, intake, parameters, facilitate,
leverage, stark, notably, "it is worth noting", "this appears to", "in today's world",
"look no further", "unlock", "elevate", "seamless", "nestled", "world-class",
"state-of-the-art" and "cutting-edge" (unless naming the actual technology).

**Empty praise (rewrite into something specific):** "friendly staff", "professional
service", "highly recommended", "great experience", "clean facilities", "knowledgeable
staff", "good clinic", "excellent service", "wonderful experience".

> Instead of *"friendly, professional staff and a great experience"* →
> *"Dr. Katy is named repeatedly for explaining results clearly, and blood-test
> results come back to WhatsApp within about two hours."*

## Brand & non-negotiables

- **ThailandClinics editorial voice.** Rankings are "ThailandClinics' editorial pick".
- **NEVER mention Locully** or any agency on the public directory. Zero exceptions.
- No hype, no stock-photo tone, no pressure. Benefit-led, evidence-first.

## Before you publish — quick check

- [ ] Opens with the answer, not a wind-up
- [ ] Every clinic/claim is specific (names, conditions, ฿, districts)
- [ ] Honest — includes the trade-off / when something else is better
- [ ] No banned words or empty praise
- [ ] British spelling, sentence-case headings
- [ ] Meta description 140–155 chars, answers the intent in the first clause
- [ ] FAQ uses `### Question`; costs in a table; updated date present
- [ ] No Locully / agency mention anywhere
